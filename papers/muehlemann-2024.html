AI Adoption and Workplace Training

Samuel Muehlemann • 2024 • IZA Working Paper
training skills Germany hiring quasi-experimental

Summary

  • Looks at AI adoption in German firms from 2019 to 2021, finding that adoption reduced training for new workers, increased the share of high-skilled hires, reduced the share of medium-skilled hires, and increased the number of apprenticeship contracts.

Abstract

2019-2021 data, AI adoption reduces the provision of continuing training for incumbent workers while increasing the share of high-skilled new hires and decreasing medium-skilled hires, thereby contributing to skill polarization.
