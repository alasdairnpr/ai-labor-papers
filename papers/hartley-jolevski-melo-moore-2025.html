The Labor Market Effects of Generative Artificial Intelligence

Jonathan Hartley, Filip Jolevski, Vitor Melo, Brendan Moore • 2025 • SSRN Working Paper
employment wages adoption survey descriptive

Summary

  • Two elements: survey of AI use in the workplace and analysis of AI's effects on wages and employment since 2022 using private job posting data and BLS wage data.
  • Previous surveys show varying AI adoption: a 2023 Pew survey found 20% teens used ChatGPT for schoolwork; a 2024 Pew survey similarly found 20% of American workers had used it at work at least once/year; and the biweekly US Census Business Trends and Outlook Survey found that 3.7% of firms used AI in Sept 2023 and 5.4% in Feb 2024.
  • This paper surveyed a nationally representative sample of U.S. workers and found LLM use at work rose from 30% in Dec 2024, to 43% in March/April 2025, to 46% in June/July 2025. Among LLM users, 1/3 said they used it every work day.
  • Their analysis of job postings and BLS data found that more AI-exposed occupations saw wage gains since late 2022, while employment levels and job postings did not change.

Abstract

Surveys adoption finding rise from 30% LLM use in Dec. 2024 to 36.7% in Sept. 2025. Small effect on wages in more exposed occupations, no significant effect on job openings or total jobs.
