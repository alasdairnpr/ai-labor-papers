Artificial Intelligence and the Labor Market

Menaka Hampole, Dimitris Papanikolaou, Lawrence D.W. Schmidt, Bryan Seegmiller • 2025 • NBER Working Paper No. 33509
employment AI exposure task based firm level quasi-experimental

Summary

Develops new measure of task-level exposure to AI from 2010 to 2023. Within-firm employment in most exposed occupations fell by ~3% relative to least exposed occupations, but economy-wide employment in those occupations rose slightly due to productivity-driven firm expansion.

Abstract

Developed task-level AI exposure measures for U.S. occupations (2010–2023) and studied firm-level outcomes. They find tasks with higher AI exposure subsequently see lower labor demand. Within firms, two factors matter: the average exposure of a job's tasks (which depresses demand) and the concentration of that exposure in a few tasks (which offsets losses by allowing workers to shift to other tasks).
