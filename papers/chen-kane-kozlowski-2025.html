The (Short-Term) Effects of LLMs on Unemployment and Earnings

Danqing Chen, Carina Kane, Austin Kozlowski, Nadav Kunievsky, James A. Evans • 2025 • arXiv preprint
wages unemployment AI exposure earnings quasi-experimental

Summary

  • Using an AI exposure measure based on Claude prompts, analyzes US unemployment rates and wages before and after the release of ChatGPT.
  • Find that the release of ChatGPT raised earnings for workers in highly exposed occupations by an average of $89 per week, with the effect concentrated in 2024 and 2025. They find no observable effect on unemployment rates.
  • Agree with the EIG finding that the most exposed occupations have consistently lower unemployment than less exposed occupations.

Abstract

Analyzed U.S. occupational outcomes in the wake of ChatGPT's release by comparing high vs. low AI-exposure occupations. They find no spike in unemployment for highly exposed jobs, but do detect a significant earnings increase for workers in high-exposure occupations after late 2022.
