Robots and Jobs: Evidence from US Labor Markets

Daron Acemoglu, Pascual Restrepo • 2020 • Journal of Political Economy
automation robots employment wages manufacturing empirical

Summary

Landmark study finding that each robot added per 1,000 workers reduced employment-to-population ratio by 0.2% and wages by 0.42%. Effects concentrated in manufacturing and among workers without college degrees.

Abstract

We study the effects of the increase in industrial robot usage between 1990 and 2007 on US local labor markets. Using a model in which robots compete against human labor in the production of different tasks, we show that robots may reduce employment and wages.
