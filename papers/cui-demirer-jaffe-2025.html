The Effects of Generative AI on High-Skilled Work: Evidence from Three Field Experiments with Software Developers

Kevin Z. Cui, Mert Demirer, Sonia Jaffe, Leon Musolff, Sida Peng, Tobias Salz • 2025 • SSRN Working Paper
productivity coding GitHub Copilot software development experimental

Summary

  • Pools three experiments randomizing access to Copilot among SWE at Microsoft (Sept 2022), Accenture (July 2023), and another Fortune 100 company (Oct 2023).
  • Use of Copilot increased weekly completed tasks (pull requests) by 26%, commits by 13.5%, and number of code compiles by 38%.
  • MSFT only: Approval rate for PRs rose 10% after Copilot adoption; other measures of quality (number of comments, number of merge conflicts, odds of approval by any reviewer) saw no significant change.
  • MSFT only: newer employees 9% more likely to adopt Copilot and more likely to continue using, juniors 5% more likely to adopt; newer employees increase output by 27-39% with Copilot, long-tenured by 8-13%; juniors increase by 21-40%, seniors by 7-16%.

Abstract

Analyzed three large RCTs at Microsoft, Accenture, and another firm (~4,867 software developers in total) where some were given GitHub Copilot (an AI code autocompletion tool). Pooled results show developers with AI completed 26% more tasks per week than the control group. Adoption and gains were higher for less-experienced developers.
