Generative AI and Labor Market Outcomes: Evidence from the United Kingdom

Bouke Klein Teeselink • 2025 • SSRN Working Paper
employment UK job postings entry level quasi-experimental

Summary

  • Combines private UK labor market data with a standard measure of AI exposure (Eloundou et al) to estimate the impact of AI since 2022 on employment, wages, pay dispersion, job seniority, and hiring dynamics.
  • Finds the most exposed firms experienced a 4.5% fall in total employment relative to firms without exposure, with the decline skewed toward junior positions.
  • The most exposed firms posted 16pp fewer job ads, with the biggest declines in technical and creative roles and small increases in customer-facing roles.
  • Highly exposed occupations saw a 23% decline in job postings, and became marginally less likely to allow remote work.
  • These effects were concentrated in high-wage jobs and firms, suggesting that LLMs may, initially at least, compress wages rather than increase inequality.

Abstract

Used U.K. firm and vacancy data (2021–2025) to study differential impacts in firms/jobs with varying exposure to LLM capabilities. Results show high-LLM-exposed firms cut total employment, especially entry-level hiring. These firms sharply pulled back on new hires in technical roles.
