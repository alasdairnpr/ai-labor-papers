Experimental evidence on the productivity effects of generative AI

Shakked Noy, Whitney Zhang • 2023 • Science
productivity writing ChatGPT skill compression experimental

Summary

  • Gave 400+ college educated professionals writing tasks, half of them prompted to use ChatGPT (2023).
  • ChatGPT group took 37% less time on tasks (17 minutes vs 27 minutes), got 0.45 SD higher evaluations. In fixed-time task, ChatGPT group scored 0.39 SD better.
  • Primarily substitution effect: 68% of treatment group submitted ChatGPT output without editing; spending longer on task after producing AI output didn't increase quality. Raw ChatGPT output scored similarly to human+AI output.
  • AI halved time spent drafting the response and doubled time spent editing. Participants were willing to pay ~0.5% of their monthly salary for ChatGPT access.
  • AI use increased job satisfaction by 0.4 SD, feelings of self-efficacy by 0.2 SD, fear of automation in subject's profession by 0.26 SD, belief that AI will boost productivity by .39 SD, and overall optimism about AI advances by 0.2 SD.
  • In follow-up survey, treatment group were more likely (33% vs 18%) to be using ChatGPT in their jobs; those who weren't mostly said it was because AI didn't have relevant context specific knowledge for their field or company.

Abstract

Ran a randomized experiment with 453 professionals doing writing tasks, with half given access to ChatGPT. ChatGPT raised productivity substantially: average task completion time fell ~40% and output quality improved ~18%. The productivity boost was larger for weaker writers, narrowing skill disparities. Many treated workers adopted ChatGPT in their real jobs afterward.
