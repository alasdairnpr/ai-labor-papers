The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on the Labor Market

Michael Webb • 2020 • Stanford Working Paper
AI exposure occupations patents skills empirical

Summary

Novel method linking AI patent text to O*NET task descriptions. Finds AI exposure is highest for well-paid, educated workers—contrasting with previous automation waves that hit middle-skill workers hardest.

Abstract

I construct a measure of the exposure of tasks to artificial intelligence by comparing the text of patents to the text of job task descriptions. I find that AI-exposed occupations are at the high end of the wage distribution.
