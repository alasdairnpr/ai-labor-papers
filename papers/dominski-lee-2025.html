Advancing AI Capabilities and Evolving Labor Outcomes

Jacob Dominski, Yong Suk Lee • 2025 • arXiv preprint
employment unemployment AI exposure CPS data quasi-experimental

Summary

  • Using a novel measure of AI exposure, they analyze CPS data to identify labor market shifts after the release of ChatGPT.
  • Finds that from 2022 to 2025, a 10 point increase (on a 0 to 100 scale) in AI exposure is associated with a 5-8pp decline in total employment and a 0.64-0.68pp increase in unemployment. A 10 point increase is also associated with small hours reductions and ~1pp decline in the share of full time (relative to part time) workers.
  • Attempt to control for post-pandemic effects on remote work.

Abstract

Constructed a dynamic AI Exposure index for occupations (using GPT-4 and Claude to assess tasks) and compared CPS outcomes before vs. after the late-2022 AI breakthrough. They find occupations with higher AI exposure saw larger declines in employment and full-time work, and bigger rises in unemployment, from late 2022 to late 2024.
