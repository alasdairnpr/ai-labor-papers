Canaries in the Coal Mine? Six Facts about the Recent Employment Effects of AI

Erik Brynjolfsson, Bharat Chandar, Ruyu Chen • 2025 • Stanford Digital Economy Lab Working Paper
employment entry level payroll data automation quasi-experimental

Summary

  • Use ADP payroll data to examine changes in the labor market since the release of generative AI tools.
  • Since 2022, early career workers in the most exposed jobs have seen a 13% decline in employment relative to less exposed fields and more experienced workers. The AI effect for early career workers showed up primarily in employment level, not wages.
  • Employment declines were concentrated in fields where AI is more likely to automate than augment human labor, which they measure by estimating whether Claude queries in an occupation substitute or complement for relevant tasks.

Abstract

Used high-frequency payroll data (ADP) to document how U.S. jobs in AI-exposed occupations have changed since generative AI's widespread adoption. They report that entry-level workers (age 22–25) in the most AI-exposed occupations experienced a 13% decline in employment relative to less-exposed groups.
