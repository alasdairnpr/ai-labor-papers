Artificial intelligence, hiring and employment: job postings evidence from Sweden

Erik Engberg, Mark Hellsten, Farrukh Javed, Magnus Lodefalk, Radka Sabolová, Sarah Schroeder • 2024 • Applied Economics Letters
employment hiring Sweden job postings descriptive

Summary

  • Combines universal Swedish employment data from 2014 - 2022 with a measure of occupational AI exposure.
  • Finds that (pre widespread LLM use), firms more exposed to AI competition hire more AI workers and use more AI services.
  • The net effect of pre-LLM AI was to increase hiring for both AI and non-AI related roles, but without substantial resulting productivity increases overall. They suggest that AI exposed firms may be using AI to augment existing roles and hire more complementary workers, rather than using it to replace workers.

Abstract

Covers 2014-2022 Swedish job postings. AI-exposed firms hire more AI-related workers. Compositional changes but no large aggregate losses.
