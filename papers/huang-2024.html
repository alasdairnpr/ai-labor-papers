The Labor Market Impact of Artificial Intelligence: Evidence from US Regions

Yueling Huang • 2024 • IMF Working Paper
employment regional manufacturing pre LLM quasi-experimental

Summary

Examines labor impact of AI in the U.S. from 2010-2021 using regional variation in AI adoption. Areas with higher AI adoption experienced larger declines in employment-to-population ratio, disproportionately borne by low-skill services and manufacturing, non-STEM workers, men, young and old workers.

Abstract

Pre-ChatGPT data from 2010-2021. Finds larger decline in employment-to-population ratio in commuting zones with higher AI adoption. Effect mainly in manufacturing and low-skilled service sectors and young+old employees. Worse for men than women.
