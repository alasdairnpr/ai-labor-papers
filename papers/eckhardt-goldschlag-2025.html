AI and Jobs: The Final Word (Until the Next One)

Sarah E. Eckhardt, Nathan Goldschlag • 2025 • EIG Report
employment unemployment CPS data AI exposure descriptive

Summary

Finds no aggregate effects of generative AI on the labor force in analysis of CPS data from 2022 to 2025. Unemployment rate has risen more for the least AI-exposed workers than for the most. Most AI-exposed quintile switches occupations more often but rate hasn't risen since 2022.

Abstract

Analyzed U.S. Current Population Survey data (2022–2025) using multiple occupation-level AI exposure indices. They find no evidence of AI-driven job losses so far. The unemployment rate rose less for the most AI-exposed quintile of workers (+0.3 pp) than for the least exposed quintile (+0.9 pp) since ChatGPT's release.
