Winners and losers of generative AI: Early Evidence of Shifts in Freelancer Demand

Ole Teutloff, Johanna Einsiedler, Otto Kässi, Fabian Braesemann, Pamela Mishkin, R. Maria del Rio-Chanona • 2025 • Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
employment freelancers skills substitution quasi-experimental

Summary

Uses freelancing platform data to categorize skills as substitutable, complementary, or unaffected by LLMs. About 10% of postings are for substitutable skills. After ChatGPT, demand rose for complementary skills and declined 20-50% for substitutable skills like writing and translation.

Abstract

LLM-based clustering shows 10% of job postings are substitutable by GenAI. GenAI cut demand for substitutable skill clusters by up to 50% in short-term roles. Demand for some complementary AI clusters like machine learning increased.
