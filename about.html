About This Database

Purpose

This database curates economics research examining how artificial intelligence and automation affect labor markets. As AI capabilities expand rapidly, understanding the empirical evidence on employment, wages, and skill demands becomes increasingly important for researchers, policymakers, and the public.

What's Included

We focus on peer-reviewed academic papers and working papers from recognized economics research institutions. Each entry includes:

  • The original abstract from the paper
  • A curated summary highlighting key findings
  • Methodology classification (empirical, theoretical, survey, etc.)
  • Topic tags for easy filtering
  • Direct links to the original source

Coverage

Topics covered include:

  • Industrial automation and robotics
  • Machine learning and AI adoption
  • Task-based models of labor markets
  • Wage effects of technological change
  • Skill-biased technical change
  • Labor market polarization
  • Worker displacement and reallocation
  • Policy responses to automation
